About This Website

Here at Caduceus, we provide you with information about COVID as well as access to a doctor if you need!

Our goal is to deliver messages from people in need of assistance directly to volunteering doctors and
let them see an immediate response.On our front page, we display some general information about COVID.
We display information like the origins of COVID,the symptoms, how to care for someone with COVID, and just to lighten
up the mood a bit, we included a few COVID memes as well.

You can connect with a doctor from anywhere around the world and talk to them about anything. Neither the identity of you or the doctor will be compromised.

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 the disease which shook the whole world is caused by a virus commonly known as coronavirus (SARS-CoV-2).

It is a respiratory disease which can even cause death. The first Covid case was reported in Wuhan, China which was reported to the WHO in Dec 2019.

Symptoms of COVID-19

COVID manifests differently in individuals; however, the infected most usually shows the following:

  • Most Common: Fever, fatigue, dry coughing
  • Less Common: Body aches, sore throat, diarrhea, headaches, loss of senses (taste or smell), rashes or discoloration, eye inflammation
  • Dangerous symptoms: Shortness of breath, chest pain, loss of speech and movement

It can take anywhere from 5-6 days to 14 days for symptoms to show.



When to see a doctor?

Immediately see a doctor if you are having any of the following:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Feel a persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • Display an inability to wake or stay awake
  • Show pale, gray, or blue-colored skin, lips, or nail beds.



Who is more likely to get severe symptoms?

People who are older and have a history of Autoimmune disease, Bone marrow transplant, Cancer,
Chronic respiratory condition, Genetic immune deficiency, such as immunosuppressive medication use,
such as for inflammatory bowel disease, HIV/AIDS, or other conditions, or Organ transplant have a higher
chance of getting more severe symptoms of COVID.



If you think you may have contracted the coronavirus, you can self check yourself using CDC's COVID self-checker.

How COVID-19 spreads?

Coronavirus is mainly spread through droplets and virus particles released into the air
an infected person breathes, talks,laughs, sings, coughs or sneezes. Larger droplets may
fall to the ground in a few seconds, but tiny infectious particles can linger in the air
and travel upto certain distances. These particles can easily accumulate in indoor places, especially
where many people are gathered and there is poor ventilation.This is why mask-wearing,
hand hygiene and physical distancing are essential to preventing COVID-19.
